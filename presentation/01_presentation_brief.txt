PROJECT TITLE
Forecasting Net Electricity Demand in Great Britain using Regional Weather + Regional Solar + National Grid Data (2020–2025)

PRESENTATION LENGTH
5 minutes total (strict).

TARGET AUDIENCE
Non‑technical but critical. They will challenge dataset validity, licensing, joins, and causality claims.

ONE‑SENTENCE SUMMARY
We combine UK‑licensed regional weather + regional solar output with national demand data to explain and forecast net electricity demand in Great Britain.

THE QUESTION (FINAL)
How do regional temperature and wind conditions, along with regional solar generation, explain and predict net electricity demand in Great Britain during a selected 6‑month window?

WHAT WE HAVE DONE SO FAR
- Collected regional solar PV generation (PV‑Live, GSP level) and converted to hourly.
- Collected national demand data with embedded wind/solar generation (NESO Historic Demand Data).
- Built a GSP mapping file (NESO GSP info) and auto‑mapped GSP points to official UK county boundaries (ONS CTYUA 2024) to match our region labels.
- Built correlation scripts (ND↔Temp, Embedded Solar↔Solar_*, Embedded Wind↔Wind10m_*).

KEY DELIVERABLES (SO FAR)
- regional solar hourly dataset (solar_energy_hourly.csv)
- GSP mapping audit (gsp_region_auto_map.csv)
- correlation tables for ND vs regional temperature, wind, solar
- documentation with dataset licenses

WHAT WE WILL SHOW
- 1–2 core EDA plots (correlation heatmap or top correlations)
- A clean pipeline diagram (data sources → mapping → merge → model)
- A modeling plan (baseline + 2 advanced models)

LIMITATIONS (OWN THEM)
- PV‑Live is estimated outturns, not meter‑exact.
- Regional mapping uses official boundaries, but GSPs are not 1:1 with counties (we document the mapping coverage).
- Time alignment must be done carefully (BST vs UTC) before modeling.

WHY THIS IS DEFENSIBLE
- All datasets are UK‑public and licensed (NESO Open Licence, CC BY 4.0, OGL).
- Mapping is transparent and auditable.
- We make predictive claims, not causal claims.
